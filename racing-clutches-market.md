<p><p>The racing clutches market features three primary types: carbon/carbon, metallic, and cerametallic. Carbon/carbon clutches excel in high-performance applications due to their lightweight and excellent heat resistance, though they are often more expensive. Metallic clutches provide durability and are suitable for a variety of racing conditions, making them a popular choice among racers. Cerametallic clutches combine characteristics of both, offering improved friction performance and thermal stability, which enhances grip and reduces wear, catering to the demands of competitive racing environments.</p></p>
