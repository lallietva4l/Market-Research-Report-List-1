<p><ul><li>Passive</li><li>Active</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Radon gas testing services are categorized into passive and active market types. Passive testing involves placing a detector in an area for a specified duration, typically 2 to 90 days, to measure radon levels without any power source. This method is simple and cost-effective. In contrast, active testing utilizes powered devices to continuously monitor radon concentrations, providing real-time results and often shorter testing periods. Both methods aim to identify radon exposure risks in residential and commercial properties, ensuring safety for occupants.</p></p>
